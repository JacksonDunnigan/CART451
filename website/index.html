CART 451 Reflections

By Jackson Dunnigan

Week 1 Response

You're a conflicted individual, a double dipped pen, crumpled newsprint and a cardstock abstraction,with the latter, your goal. Your soul thrives on unspooling unconventional thought, where words and intent symphonically merge to chaotic colors and shapes of unspoken stories of people you can vaguely assign to faces or feelings. You speak in the language of the abstract, weaving narratives that challenge convention and defy the ordinary. Eclecticism transcending boundaries, finding beauty in the unconventional and solace in the dissonant. In your journey, you invite others to explore with you, uncovering the mysteries of this captivating world of boundless expression.

Week 3 Response

Wikipedia embodies the pinnacle of crowdsourcing, illustrating the potential of decentralized collaboration to curate and share knowledge on an unprecedented scale. It is a testament to the collective intelligence of a global community, pooling their diverse expertise to construct a vast, ever-evolving encyclopedia. Jeff Howe's article "The Rise of Crowdsourcing" provides a broader lens on the phenomenon, elucidating how crowdsourcing extends beyond knowledge creation, encompassing diverse applications such as problem-solving, innovation, and collective creativity. On the other hand, Lauren Lee McCarthy's work often employs crowdsourcing and technology to delve into the dynamics of human interaction and intimacy. While Wikipedia stands as an exemplary crowdsourced knowledge repository, McCarthy's art highlights the potential of crowdsourcing in eliciting critical reflections on societal connections and the influence of technology on contemporary human experiences. Both Wikipedia and McCarthy's work underscore the transformative impact of crowdsourcing, whether in knowledge creation or in redefining social and artistic paradigms.
